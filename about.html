Research

My dissertation topic was "The Role of Disks in the Angular Momentum Evolution of Young, Low-Mass Stars" and was completed in August 2012 at Vanderbilt University, with the guidance of my adviser Dr. Keivan G. Stassun. My interest in T-Tauri stars centers on the "disk-locking" theory to explain the regulation of its angular momentum.

I worked in producing spectral energy distributions (or SEDs - this tells us how much light is emitted at each wavelength) using a Monte-Carlo algorithm to look at the infrared signature of young stars with disks (T-Tauri stars). My goal was to test the "disk-locking" theory, which attempts to explain the angular momentum loss of these young stars.

Very young stars (Pre-main sequence or PMS) rotate very rapidly; as they contract and become more compact, they should spin up to near-break up speed assuming that the principle of conservation of angular momentum is upheld. By the time they get to the main sequence, however, they possess a spin much like that of our own sun (about one rotation per day). What causes them to lose their initial angular momentum? And does "disk-locking" sufficiently explain this observed phenomenon?

Personal Interests

As a child, I remember looking up at the stars and wondering what was beyond the skies. I've always enjoyed looking at the constellations, my favourite being the Little Dipper. Until I discovered that the Little Dipper was actually the Pleiades.

My goal going through graduate school was to eventually work with NASA in whatever capacity I could. Today, at the Space Telescope Science Institute, I consider that goal conquered, but now replaced by another; to discover!. It is to this cause that I dedicate my time here at the Institute for the years to come, and I am excited to lend my talents towards the goals of the Institute.

Born on the beautiful island of Puerto Rico, my ideal outdoor environment is the sun, sea and sand, with palm trees in the background. Growing up in the Caribbean (on four different islands) have given me an appreciation for the simple, slow life where it is possible to hear the steel pan sounds of rain on a tin roof.

I enjoy being a husband and a father; spending time with family is one of the truly simple pleasures of life! I am an avid fan of music, revel in experimental homecooked meals (of the Caribbean variety, of course), and occupy myself by attempting to fix something broken or make something new. Other interests include reading SciFi and Children's Fantasy books (I never really grew up), electronics (of all kinds), and soon, Homebrewing.
